Survive the Skellies

Survive the Skellies is a Survivors-like game built in Unreal Engine 5.6 for my C++ for Designers unit at University. The goal was to use C++ in Unreal Engine to create a designer friendly game, with classes that could be used to create actors with functionality in blueprint. I designed and implemented systems for a survivors-like game, weapons, enemy spawning in waves, EXP and level up systems and stat scaling.

View GitHub Repository

Waves and Enemy Spawning

Enemy spawning is handled through my Enemy Spawn Manager class. This class handled what enemies to spawn, how many to spawn, how frequently and the chance of the enemy to spawn. I designed it in this was so that I could add as many enemies as I wanted. However, I stuck to 2 for the sake of simplicity.
When a wave is completed there is a delay before the next wave spawns. As the waves increment, the amount of enemies spawned in the next wave increments and the enemies scale in power, they become bulkier and deal more damage to the player.

Weapons

Survivors-like games have a wide variety of weapons to choose for players. For this project, I implemented 3 weapons:

Aura Weapon
Periodically pulses, dealing damage to all enemies caught in the pulse.

Projectile Weapon
Periodically fires a projectile at the closest enemy within its range.

Lightning Weapon
Periodically strikes a random enemy with lightning.

EXP and Leveling

As the player defeats enemies, EXP is dropped. When the player collects enough EXP, a level up is triggered. The player is then given the opportunity to upgrade 1 of their stats.

Max Health
Increases the maximum health of the player.

Damage
Increases the damage dealt by weapons.

Armour
Reduces incoming damage by a flat amount.

Cooldown
Increases the rate at which weapons fire.

Movement Speed
Increases the speed at which the player can move.

Size
Increases the range at which weapons target enemies.
