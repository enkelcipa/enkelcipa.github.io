Project Interesting Mundane Tasks

Project Interesting Mundane Tasks (IMT) is a job simulator game made in Unreal Engine 5.6 for my Final Year Project at University. This game is based around the question of "How do I make mundane tasks interesting and engaging?".

This project allowed me to explore how developers retain player's attention when the task at hand is uninteresting and boring.

View GitHub Repository

Tasks

The main gameplay loop involves the player running around the map completing various tasks that are assigned randomly. These tasks require the player to complete various minigames to complete the task.

These minigames are comprised of:

Connecting a New PC to the Network
Connecting Wires

Diagnosing System Errors
Quick Time Events

Accepting Mail Delivery
Press and Hold

Smoking
Dragging Objects, Quick Time Events, Press and Hold

Making Coffee
Dragging Objects

Going to the Bathroom
Press and Hold

Resources

In addition to various minigames, I implemented 3 resource bars that the player had to manage in order to be able to complete the game:

Alertness
Drains slowly as time passes.
The player's movement speed is depended on how full the Alertness Bar is.
When the bar is fully depleted the player becomes incapacitated and a game over is displayed.

Shortcomings
Drains slowly as time passes.
This bar acts as a multiplier for the Stress Bar.
The more it fills, the faster the Stress Bar fills.

Stress
Increases slowly as time passes.
Certain actions cause this bar to increase.
The player is stopped at random intervals the more this bar is filled.
When this bar is filled, the player becomes enraged and a game over is displayed.

These resources add to the engagement of the player as now they have to juggle the completion of tasks alongside the management of resources that could cause them to fail entirely.
